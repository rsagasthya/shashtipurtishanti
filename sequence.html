Sequence of Events

From 8 am

  • Kalasha Sthapana

    Kalasha Sthapana, or the establishment of the sacred pot, marks the beginning of many Hindu rituals and ceremonies. A Kalasha, often filled with water and topped with mango leaves and a coconut, symbolizes the presence of deities and the cosmos. It invokes divine blessings for the occasion.

  • Ganesha Pooja

    This ritual involves worshiping Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles, before starting any auspicious event. It seeks his blessings for a successful, smooth, and prosperous undertaking.

  • Punyaha

    Punyaha is a purification ritual performed to sanctify the space, participants, and surroundings before a major ceremony. Holy water, mantras, and other rituals are used to cleanse and invite divine energy.

  • Paada Pooja

    In this ritual, the feet of revered individuals, such as elders, spiritual leaders, or deities, are washed as a mark of respect and devotion. It symbolizes humility and the offering of service.

  • Naandi

    Naandi is a ritual where the family seeks blessings from ancestors and deities to ensure the success and prosperity of the upcoming event. Special prayers and offerings are made.
From 9Min am

  • Navagraha Homa

    This sacred fire ritual is dedicated to the nine planetary deities (Navagrahas) in Hindu astrology. It aims to appease these celestial influences to promote harmony, prosperity, and good fortune.

  • Mrityunjaya Homa

    This powerful fire ritual is performed to invoke Lord Shiva's blessings for protection against untimely death, severe ailments, and misfortune. The chant of the Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra is central to this homa.

  • Ugra Mrityunjaya Homa

    An intensified version of the Mrityunjaya Homa, this ritual is conducted to address extreme adversities, critical health conditions, or serious obstacles. It seeks Lord Shiva's potent protection and grace.

  • Ayush Homa

    Ayush Homa is performed to pray for longevity, good health, and overall well-being. It is commonly conducted on birthdays or special events.

  • Avabrutha Snaana

    This is a ceremonial bath taken at the conclusion of a significant Vedic ritual or ceremony. It symbolizes the purification and successful completion of the event.

  • Mangalya Dharana

    This ritual is a central part of Hindu ceremonies where the husband ties the sacred mangalsutra (thread or necklace) around the wife's neck. It signifies the marital bond and commitment between the couple.

  • Dampati Pooja

    This involves worshiping a married couple, usually considered representatives of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati or Lakshmi and Vishnu. It seeks their blessings for harmony, prosperity, and marital bliss.
